Sport as a universal form of help

Everyone needs exercise

Sports and physical activity are universal forms of assistance that can improve the overall well-being of any remote worker, regardless of what exactly is the source of their problems. In home office mode one performs intellectual work. For the vast majority, this simply means working in a sitting position using a computer. Sitting in one position for 8 hours five days a week puts a lot of strain on our bodies. Human bodies are designed to move and remote workers, regardless of the problems they might feel, are in desperate need of physical activity. Sport, not just professional sports, has a healing effect on our psyche. Even patients in deep depression are advised to for example take daily walks as a form of supportive therapy.

Physical activity for you

Therefore, I encourage you to find a form of physical activity that works for you. You don't have to suddenly start running like a pro. On the contrary - don't do it, because you will only harm yourself. You can even start with short but regular walks. Look for a form of sport that makes you happy or is at least acceptable for you. If you are not sure what activity you can undertake without injuring yourself and which form of sport will be best for you, consult a personal trainer or physiotherapist.

Here's how sports and physical activity can help you:

Improvement of physical health:

  • Strengthening muscles and bones.
  • Improving cardiovascular fitness.
  • Weight control.

Improvement of mental health:

  • Stress reduction: Exercise contributes to the release of endorphins, which improve mood and reduce stress levels.
  • Increased energy levels: Regular physical activity improves the body's overall fitness, which translates into higher energy levels throughout the day.
  • Improved concentration and productivity: exercising improves blood flow to the brain, which can positively affect concentration, memory and cognitive abilities.

Increase of motivation and self-discipline:

  • Planning your day: Regular physical activity requires planning, which can help you better manage your time.
  • Goal achievement: Achieving physical activity goals can inspire motivation and feelings of satisfaction in other areas of life, including work.

Improvement of social relations:

  • Group exercises: Participating in group activities or exercising together online can improve social relationships, build a sense of community and give opportunity to make new friends.

Preventing health problems associated with sedentary work:

  • Reducing the risk of spinal problems: Regular exercise, including those aimed at strengthening back muscles and improving posture, can prevent back pain and circulation problems resulting from prolonged sitting.
  • Improved cardiovascular circulation: Physical activity promotes blood circulation, which is important when sitting in front of a computer for long periods of time.

Creation of healthy habits:

  • Regular breaks for exercising: Introducing short exercise breaks during the workday can help maintain work-life balance and improve overall well-being.
