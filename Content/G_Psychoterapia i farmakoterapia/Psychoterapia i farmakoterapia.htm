Psychotherapy and pharmacotherapy

Do not be alone with your problems

Psychotherapy and pharmacotherapy can significantly help remote workers cope with a variety of mental health problems that arise, for example, from feelings of loneliness and isolation, stress, medications or difficulties in separating work and private life. If the lowered mood is already very troublesome, symptoms of depression, panic attacks, insomnia or other serious ailments appear, do not wait until it gets worse, seek immediate professional help from a specialist: a psychologist, psychiatrist or psychotherapist.

Working with these people can help significantly not only if we are troubled with problems that qualify us to start pharmacotherapy already. Working on introducing new habits, learning to make new friends or increasing self-awareness as to our needs in our professional lives are just some aspects in which the assistance of a psychotherapist can prove invaluable. Remember that you don't have to be alone with your problems.

Here are some examples of issues that psychotherapy and pharmacotherapy can help you with:

Psychotherapy

Stress and anxiety management:
  • Therapists can teach relaxation and mindfulness techniques that help reduce stress and anxiety. They also assist in identifying and changing negative thoughts and behaviours with which we unconsciously drive ourselves into even greater stress and anxiety.
Improvement of emotional health:
  • Regular therapy sessions offer a space to express emotions and discuss problems, which brings relief and improves well-being. Therapists also help develop healthy coping strategies, which builds agency and satisfaction.
Increasing productivity and motivation:
  • Therapists can help set realistic career goals and teach how to plan. Psychotherapy will also contribute to the accurate identification and resolution of problems associated with remote work, such as procrastination and time management difficulties.
Improvement of interpersonal relations:
  • Therapists can help develop communication and social skills, which is especially important in the context of remote work. During therapy, for example, you will learn conflict management skills, which is useful, among other things, in the case of tensions in professional relationships.

Pharmacotherapy

  • Treating depression, anxiety disorders and mood swings.
  • Medication can also help with for example concentration problems associated with ADHD or sleep disorders.
  • Treating sleeping problems that often accompany remote work.
